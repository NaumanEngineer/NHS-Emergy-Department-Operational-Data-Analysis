
================================================================================
LIMITATIONS & CAVEATS
================================================================================

IMPORTANT CAVEATS FOR STAKEHOLDER PRESENTATION:

1. SYNTHETIC DATA LIMITATIONS:
   ✓ This analysis uses synthetic (simulated) data for portfolio demonstration
   ✓ Real NHS data may show different patterns
   ✓ Use this as framework for real data analysis, not operational decisions
   ✓ Validate findings with actual organizational data before implementation

2. TEMPORAL DYNAMICS NOT CAPTURED:
   ✗ Analysis uses cross-sectional correlations (single year snapshot)
   ✗ Causal lags not modeled (e.g., staffing changes take time to affect occupancy)
   ✓ Would need time-series analysis with real data for intervention planning

3. INDIVIDUAL-LEVEL vs SYSTEM-LEVEL EFFECTS:
   ✗ Correlations computed at episode level, not trust/department level
   ✗ May mask important organizational differences
   ✓ Real analysis should stratify by organizational unit

4. CONFOUNDING NOT FULLY EXPLORED:
   ✗ Occupancy ↔ ED time: May be bidirectional
     (Long ED times cause high occupancy, OR high occupancy causes long waits)
   ✗ Cannot determine causation from correlation alone
   ✓ Requires experimental/quasi-experimental design or advanced modeling

5. MISSING VARIABLES:
   ✗ Not measured: Bed turnover time, discharge planning quality, patient acuity
   ✗ Not measured: Staff expertise, process efficiency, equipment availability
   ✗ These could all confound observed relationships
   ✓ Real analysis should include these variables

6. OUTLIER HANDLING:
   ✓ Flagged outliers rather than removing them (preserves data integrity)
   ⚠️  But outliers may represent different operational regimes
      (Normal ops: 70% occupancy, Crisis ops: 95% occupancy)
   ✓ Future analysis should model different regimes separately

WHAT WE CAN CONFIDENTLY CLAIM:

✓ Strong statistical relationships exist (p < 0.001)
✓ Relationships are robust to outlier removal
✓ Patterns consistent with operational theory
✓ Effect sizes are clinically meaningful

WHAT WE CANNOT CLAIM (Without Further Evidence):

✗ Causal relationships (only correlation)
✗ Generalization to other trusts/regions
✗ Specific quantified impacts (differ by context)
✗ Ranking of intervention priorities (depends on costs/feasibility)

WHEN PRESENTING TO STAKEHOLDERS:

DO:
  ✓ Show confidence intervals (demonstrate precision)
  ✓ Mention effect sizes (ρ = -0.85 is stronger than ρ = -0.40)
  ✓ Acknowledge limitations honestly (builds credibility)
  ✓ Frame as "insights to investigate further" not "proof of causation"

DON'T:
  ✗ Overstate confidence ("this WILL reduce waits by X minutes")
  ✗ Claim causation ("staffing CAUSES occupancy changes")
  ✗ Ignore confounders ("occupancy is THE constraint")
  ✗ Pretend synthetic data are real ("this is what happens in your ED")

EXAMPLE PHRASE:

"The data suggest a strong association between staffing and occupancy
(ρ = -0.72, p < 0.001), consistent with the hypothesis that adequate
staffing improves throughput and occupancy management. We recommend
further investigation with your operational data to confirm whether this
relationship holds in your specific context."
