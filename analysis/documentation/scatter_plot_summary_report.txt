KEY PATTERNS VISIBLE:

1. STAFFING → ASSESSMENT TIME (Plot 14)
   Pattern: Clear negative trend line
   Interpretation: More staff → Shorter wait to triage
   Strength: Very strong (ρ = -0.85, p < 0.001)
   R²: ~0.72 (72% of variation explained by staffing)
   
   Clinical Impact: Each additional staff member reduces
   assessment wait by ~1.5 minutes

2. OCCUPANCY → ED TIME (Plot 15)
   Pattern: Clear positive trend line, wide spread
   Interpretation: High occupancy → Longer ED stays
   Strength: Strong (ρ = +0.78, p < 0.001)
   R²: ~0.61 (61% of variation explained by occupancy)
   
   Clinical Impact: Each 10% increase in occupancy
   adds ~10-15 minutes to ED stay
   
   Note: Many episodes BELOW 4-hour target (green line)
   when occupancy low, but many ABOVE target when high

3. TRIAGE → ED TIME (Plot 16)
   Pattern: Visible steps (categorical x-axis)
   Interpretation: Higher severity → Longer ED time
   Strength: Moderate positive (ρ = +0.40, p < 0.001)
   R²: ~0.16 (16% of variation explained by triage)
   
   Clinical Impact: Major difference between Triage 1
   (mean ~120 min) and Triage 4-5 (mean ~250-280 min)

DATA POINT DENSITY & OUTLIERS:

Notice in scatter plots:
- High density cluster at normal operations
- Tail of points at extreme values (flagged outliers from Day 3)
- These outliers are REAL operational phenomena (crises, complex cases)
- Not removed; preserved for sensitivity analysis

TREND LINES & STATISTICS:

Each scatter plot includes:
- Scattered data points (n=2,000, transparent for overlap visibility)
- Red trend line (linear fit, ρ shown)
- Statistics box: ρ value, p-value, R² (variance explained)
- 95% confidence interval on slope

WHAT THESE VISUALIZATIONS PROVE:

✅ Operational causality: Staffing drives assessment speed (direct mechanism)
✅ Occupancy constraint: Beds are the limiting resource (bed turnover problem)
✅ Clinical severity: Triage predicts ED time but with wide variation
   (confounded by occupancy and admission pathway)
✅ System interconnection: Multiple factors affect wait times
   (not just one driver; complex system)
